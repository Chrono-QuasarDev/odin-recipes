Waakye Stew Recipe

Waakye Stew

Waakye stew is a rich, savory, and deep red tomato-based stew that is the quintessential accompaniment to the Ghanaian rice and bean dish, waakye. Unlike simpler stews, waakye stew is layered with flavor through a multi-step cooking process.

Ingredients

For the protein:

  • 1 kg goat meat, beef, or a mix of meats (including cow skin or tripe, if desired), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger
  • 2–3 cloves garlic
  • 1–2 Scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
  • 1 tsp mixed spices (e.g., coriander, cumin, paprika)
  • Salt to taste

For the stew base:

  • 1/2 cup vegetable or groundnut oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1 small can (about 150g) tomato paste
  • 700g fresh, ripe tomatoes, blended
  • 1–2 Scotch bonnet peppers, blended with the tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground dried shrimp powder (optional, but recommended for authentic flavor)
  • 1 tsp mixed spices
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • Stock from steaming the meat
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and cut your meat into desired sizes
  2. In a pot, combine the meat, chopped onion, ginger, garlic, Scotch bonnet, mixed spices, and salt. Add a small amount of water, just enough to prevent the pot from burning.
  3. Steam the meat over medium heat until it is tender.
  4. Remove the cooked meat from the pot and set it aside. Reserve the flavorful meat stock, straining it to remove any bits.
  5. In a large pot or saucepan, heat the oil.
  6. Fry the meat in the hot oil until it is browned and has some texture. Remove the fried meat and set it aside.
  7. Reduce the heat and add the thinly sliced onions to the same oil. Fry until they are soft and caramelized, which should take about 10–15 minutes.
  8. Push the caramelized onions to the side of the pot and add the tomato paste to the center.
  9. Fry the tomato paste, stirring constantly, until it darkens in color. This process removes the raw, sour taste and gives the stew a deeper flavor.
  10. Add the blended fresh tomatoes and peppers to the pot, stirring to combine them with the tomato paste and onions.
  11. Continue to fry the tomato mixture, stirring regularly, until the oil separates and rises to the top. This indicates that the water has evaporated and the stew base is well-cooked.
  12. Add the reserved meat stock, shrimp powder, bay leaves, and mixed spices to the pot. Stir well.
  13. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for at least 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  14. Add the fried meat to the stew and stir to coat. Simmer for another 5–10 minutes.
  15. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, if needed.
  16. Serve the rich, dark stew hot over your waakye.
